Resources

The following resources including clinical guidelines, training tools, monitoring and evaluation tools, and publications are made available to you for download to supplement and enhance your online learning.

Clinical Guidelines

Consolidated Guidelines on the Use of Antiretroviral Drugs for Treating and Preventing HIV Infection: Recommendations for a Public Health Approach Source: World Health Organization Publication Date: June 30, 2013 The 2013 Consolidated guidelines on the use of antiretroviral drugs for treating and preventing HIV infection provide guidance on the diagnosis of human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) infection, the care of people living with HIV and the use of antiretroviral (ARV) drugs for treating and preventing HIV infection. http://www.who.int/hiv/pub/guidelines/arv2013/download/en/ An ICAP Grand Rounds Webinar Update on the 2013 WHO Guidelines may be viewed online: http://icap.columbia.edu/news-events/events/detail/update-on-2013-who-guidelines Clinical Management of HIV in Children and Adults: Malawi Integrated Guidelines for Providing HIV Services Source: Malawi Ministry of Health Publication Date: 2011 Malawi integrated guidelines offer guidance for providing HIV services in: antenatal care, maternity care, under 5 clinics, family planning clinics, exposed infant/pre-ART clinics, and ART clinics http://apps.who.int/medicinedocs/documents/s18802en/s18802en.pdf Training Tools Campus to Clinic Mentoring Guide Source: ICAP Publication Date: 2011 The Campus to Clinic training curriculum was developed by ICAP in 2011 to mentor nurse educators in South Africa with expanded knowledge in HIV care and treatment and teaching methodologies so that they may adequately prepare graduating nurses for the demands of clinical work in the provision of HIV care and treatment. The curriculum includes 36 hours of training over 9 modules of approximately 4 hours each. Topics include adult teaching and learning methods, HIV counseling and testing, adult care and treatment, pediatric care and treatment, prevention of mother-to-child transmission, and sexual and reproductive health services. The curriculum includes a Facilitator Guide and Participant Manual. http://icap.columbia.edu/resources/detail/campus-to-clinic-manual Improving Retention, Adherence and Psychosocial Support within PMTCT Services: A Toolkit for Health Workers Source: ICAP Publication Date: 2011 ICAP developed this toolkit in 2011 to aid multidisciplinary care teams in providing women, families, and babies with increased support for retention, adherence, and psychosocial wellbeing throughout the continuum of PMTCT care. The toolkit consists of 6 components: 1) an overview and introduction to the toolkit; 2) an Adaptation guide; 3) an implementation workshop curriculum, including a trainer manual, slide set, and participant manual; 4) PMTCT counseling cue cards; 5) adaptable templates of forms and guides to support adherence and retention; and 6) an educational video. This training manual is available in English and French. http://icap.columbia.edu/resources/detail/prevention-of-mother-to-child-transmission-pmtct-of-hiv-resources Monitoring and Evaluation Tools Three interlinked patient monitoring systems for HIV care/ART, MCH/PMTCT and TB/HIV: standardized minimum data set and illustrative tools Source: World Health Organization Publication Date: 2012 The patient monitoring guidelines for HIV care and ART outline a minimum data set and accompanying generic tools for data collection and reporting. More than 29 countries now use an adaptation of this generic system to support their HIV service provision and to provide important program indicators, with increasing availability of core indicators from a routine, harmonized national system. The three interlinked patient monitoring systems support integrated service provision, follow up of mother-infant pair and monitoring of key TB-related and pediatric variables. The MCH minimum data set includes all routine core maternal and infant variables plus key HIV-related variables. The generic ART cohort report has been simplified and the cross-sectional quarterly report is now integrated to collect not only HIV indicators, but also key PMTCT, MCH and TB/HIV indicators. The generic tools are illustrative, for country adaptation. https://www.dropbox.com/s/byddux2zcnbh9l9/patient%20cards%20and%20registers.pdf?dl=0 Publications Global Plan Towards the Elimination of New HIV Infections Among Children by 2015 and Keeping Their Mothers Alive Source: UNAIDS Publication Date: 2011 The Global Plan was developed through a consultative process by a high level Global Task Team convened by UNAIDS and co-chaired by UNAIDS Executive Director Michel Sidibé and United States Global AIds Coordinator Ambassador Eric Goosby. It brought together 25 countries and 30 civil society, private sector, networks of people living with HIV and international organizations to chart a roadmap to achieving this goal by 2015. This plan covers all low- and middle-income countries, but focuses on the 22 countries* with the highest estimated numbers of pregnant women living with HIV. Exceptional global and national efforts are needed in these countries that are home to nearly 90% of pregnant women living with HIV in need of services. Intensified efforts are also needed to support countries with low HIV prevalence and concentrated epidemics to reach out to all women and children at risk of HIV with the services that they need. The Global Plan supports and reinforces the development of costed country-driven national plans. *Angola, Botswana, Burundi, Cameroon, Chad, Côte d’Ivoire, Democratic Republic of the Congo, Ethiopia, Ghana, India, Kenya, Lesotho, Malawi, Mozambique, Namibia, Nigeria, South Africa, Swaziland, Uganda, United Republic of Tanzania, Zambia and Zimbabwe. http://www.unaids.org/en/media/unaids/contentassets/documents/unaidspublication/2011/20110609_JC2137_Global-Plan-Elimination-HIV-Children_en.pdf 2013 Progress Report on the Global Plan Towards the Elimination of New HIV Infections Among Children by 2015 and Keeping Their Mothers Alive Source: UNAIDS Publication Date: 2013 This report presents the interim progress made by 21 countries in sub-Saharan Africa toward meeting the Global Plan Towards the Elimination of New HIV Infections Among Children by 2015 and some of the challenges they face in meeting the agreed targets for 2015. http://www.unaids.org/en/media/unaids/contentassets/documents/unaidspublication/2013/20130625_progress_global_plan_en.pdf
